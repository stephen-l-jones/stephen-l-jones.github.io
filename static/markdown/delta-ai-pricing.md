###### Updated March 2026

Delta Airlines, one of America’s largest carriers, is piloting an AI-powered pricing system. The system, developed by Israeli startup [Fetcherr](https://www.fetcherr.io/), moves beyond traditional dynamic pricing toward individualized fare-setting. Delta used the system for small fraction of tickets in 2025, but it plans to eventually apply the approach across its network. 

Where legacy revenue management adjusted prices based on demand and timing, Delta’s new system aims to tailor fares to individual customers based on behavioral signals the company has not fully disclosed. Delta insists the system relies on aggregated market data⁠⁠—route demand, competitive pricing, weather, and macroeconomic conditions⁠—and explicitly denies using personal data to set individualized prices. 

Critics are unconvinced, pointing to Delta’s own president describing the tool as delivering “a price available to you, the individual.” They are concerned that booking history, loyalty status, device type, and inferred willingness to pay could quietly find their way into the algorithm. Consumer advocates, privacy scholars, and members of Congress have labeled the practice “surveillance pricing.” 

Rivals are watching closely, and some are exploring similar systems. Yet whether the strategy will deliver on its early promise remains far from certain.
