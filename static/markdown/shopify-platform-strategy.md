###### Updated March 2026

For most of its history, Shopify built tools for online sellers⁠⁠—storefronts, inventory management, payments processing, lending, and shipping. Consumers never knew they were interacting with Shopify infrastructure beneath the surface. But that invisibility carried a strategic cost. Shopify owned no consumer relationships, so it couldn’t generate demand for its merchants. A merchant on Amazon benefited from hundreds of millions of consumers who opened Amazon with intent to buy. A merchant on Shopify had to generate their own traffic at a significant and rising cost. Between 2013 and 2022 alone, the cost to acquire an e-commerce customer rose from $13 to $29, driven by rising ad prices and the growing dominance of large platform retailers. Shopify provided the store, but it couldn’t fill it.

Beginning in 2020, Shopify began building a consumer-facing layer on top of its merchant platform. The [Shop app](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/online-sales-channels/shop) lets consumers track orders and follow their favorite brands. [Shop Pay](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/payments/shop-pay) lets them check out with one tap⁠—a convenience that 150 million consumers, by 2026, had opted into.

Shopify wants to make the shopping experience within its ecosystem even better, and the competitive environment has intensified that goal. Amazon continues to tighten its grip on consumers through Prime. Walmart has turned its physical footprint and Walmart+ membership into an omnichannel behemoth. And TikTok Shop reached $26 billion in the first half of 2025 alone.

But the question of how far and how fast to push its platform remains open. Three broad strategic directions have emerged. First, Shopify could extend Shop Pay beyond Shopify’s merchant base, making it available across the internet like PayPal or Apple Pay. Second, it could invest in making the Shop app a consumer destination, creating a richer product discovery experience with native social and creator commerce tools that compete directly with TikTok Shop and Instagram Shopping. Third, it could scale [Shop Cash]⁠(https://help.shop.app/en/shop/shop-cash)⁠—Shopify’s consumer rewards program⁠—into a full consumer loyalty network, with bigger rewards and broader product eligibility, making Shopify storefronts the default destination.
